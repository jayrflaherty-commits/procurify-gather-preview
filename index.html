🔥 "Emergency PO After Hours"

Urgent purchases at 6–10pm when approvers are unavailable. Teams use personal cards or email chains as workarounds. A mobile-first emergency approval flow with pre-set spending limits could capture this need. Major retention lever for mid-market.

⚡ "New Hire Tech Stack Setup"

Onboarding triggers 8–12 software license purchases per hire. Currently handled via ad-hoc Slack messages to IT and finance. Templated procurement bundles with auto-approval would save 2.4 hours per hire. Differentiator vs. Airbase and Ramp.

📊 "Quarter-End Budget Scramble"

Last 5 days of quarter see 3.2× spike in purchase requests as teams rush to use remaining budget. Finance teams need a dashboard view of remaining budget by department with projected spend-down. Feature gap across all competitors.

Buyer Archetypes
📐 Process Architects (26%)

Want configurable approval workflows, audit trails, and compliance controls. Will run a 90-day eval. Over-index on Coupa but get burned by complexity. Procurify's sweet spot if positioned right.

🔥 Chaos Escapees (31%)

Hit a breaking point — audit finding, embarrassing budget overrun, or CEO mandate. Need a solution deployed in weeks, not months. Fastest path-to-close. Value speed and simplicity over feature depth.

📊 Data-Hungry CFOs (24%)

Primary need is spend visibility and real-time reporting. Less interested in PO workflows, more interested in dashboards. Currently cobbling together data from QuickBooks + spreadsheets. Want a "single pane of glass."

🛡️ Risk Minimizers (19%)

Triggered by compliance pressure — SOX prep, board requirements, or investor due diligence. Will pay premium for audit-ready controls. Most receptive to case studies and peer validation.
